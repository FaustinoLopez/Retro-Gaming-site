Genre Matrix

What Each Era Did Best

Platformers

NES and SNES tuned movement physics so tightly that modern indie platformers still mirror them.

Key picks: Super Mario Bros. 3, Super Mario World, Donkey Kong Country 2

RPG Giants

SNES and PS1 built the dramatic JRPG template through pixel art, CD audio, and long-form storytelling.

Key picks: Chrono Trigger, Final Fantasy VI, Final Fantasy VII

Action + Adventure

Z-targeting, lock-on combat, and guided 3D exploration accelerated on N64 and PS2.

Key picks: Ocarina of Time, Devil May Cry, God of War II

Local Multiplayer

N64 normalized four-player sessions at home, then PS2 carried the party with sports and racing staples.

Key picks: Mario Kart 64, GoldenEye 007, NBA Street Vol. 2

Portable Mastery

Game Boy Color and GBA made serious RPG, strategy, and action sessions possible on commutes.

Key picks: Pokemon Crystal, Advance Wars, Metroid Fusion

Horror + Atmosphere

CD consoles let developers lean into voice lines, ambient sound, and cinematic camera framing.

Key picks: Resident Evil 2, Silent Hill 2, Fatal Frame

Collector Corner

Own Hardware Without Chaos

Buy Smart Checklist

  • Confirm power adapters and video cables are original or high-quality replacements.
  • Request test photos showing boot screen, controller ports, and cartridge or disc reads.
  • Watch for yellowed shells, cracked hinges, and stripped screws on handheld battery doors.

Preservation Routine

  • Store cartridges vertically in dry rooms and avoid attic heat swings.
  • Clean contacts with isopropyl alcohol and lint-free swabs before deep troubleshooting.
  • Swap aging save batteries in carts early to avoid save data loss.

Modern Play Methods

  • Original hardware for authenticity and CRT-era response timing.
  • FPGA systems for high compatibility with modern displays.
  • Emulation for convenience, save states, and broader accessibility options.
